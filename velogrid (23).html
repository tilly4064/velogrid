About
A visual support tool for cycling infrastructure planning.

VeloGrid layers Brisbane's cycling infrastructure datasets onto one interactive map — bikeways, crash history, road hierarchy, crowdsourced safety perceptions, and electric micromobility trip patterns. By making these datasets visible together, it helps planners, researchers, and curious riders understand where the network is strong, where gaps exist, and where investment could have the greatest impact on safe cycling expansion.

Click any segment for the details behind it, follow the crash hotspots to identify high-risk corridors, and overlay the micromobility trip data to see where riders are already moving — with or without formal infrastructure.

Built on live open data from Brisbane City Council and the Queensland Government. No accounts, no tracking — just the map.
Data sources
Bikeway sections
Loaded live from the Brisbane City Council ArcGIS FeatureServer API.

Brisbane boundary
Loaded from the Queensland Government ArcGIS REST service for local government boundaries.

Crash data
Loaded live from the Queensland Government CKAN DataStore API. The app first filters to Brisbane City records, then keeps records where Count_Unit_Bicycle is at least 1.

Road hierarchy
Loaded from the Brisbane City Council Opendatasoft Explore API (City Plan 2014 — Road hierarchy overlay). The dataset contains ~41,750 road segments classified by route_type (Motorway / Arterial / Suburban / District / Neighbourhood / local). Hidden by default; toggle on for road-classification context. Note: City Plan 2014 reflects planned hierarchy and is updated infrequently — it may not match current road conditions.

Bicycle racks
Loaded from the BCC Opendatasoft API (Bicycle Rack locations dataset). Covers CBD parking infrastructure only — not a comprehensive Brisbane-wide inventory. Hidden by default.

Parks
Loaded from the BCC Opendatasoft API (Park Locations dataset). Polygons for council-managed parks across the LGA. Hidden by default since the layer is dense.

BikeSpot 2023
Crowdsourced cyclist survey (CRomero / BikeSpot 2023). Cyclists dropped pins marking locations they perceive as safe or unsafe, with reasons. No public API — bundled as a pre-processed local file filtered to Brisbane (1,337 of 15,080 national points). This is perceived safety, not measured risk: participation was voluntary and self-selected, and unsafe spots vastly outnumber safe ones because people were more motivated to flag problems. Interpret as community sentiment, not a risk model. Hidden by default.

Map tiles
CARTO Positron (light) raster tiles through MapLibre GL JS.
